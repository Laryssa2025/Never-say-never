Never say never

"Never say never" who created this phrase?

The phrase "Never say never" doesn't have a single known creator, though it's often mistakenly attributed to Charles Dickens's The Pickwick Papers. The phrase was in use in the late 19th century, with a 1911 song by Seymour Furth and other early 20th-century music hall songs using the expression, suggesting it was a popular idiom by then rather than a new invention.

Charles Dickens:

  • His writing includes books such as Oliver Twist and A Christmas Carol - books that are still very widely read today.
  • He wrote about things that many people before him had avoided writing about, like the lives of poorer people.
  • Charles Dickens is one of Britain's most famous authors.
